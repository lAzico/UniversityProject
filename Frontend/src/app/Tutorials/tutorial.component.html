Blockchain Explorer Tutorial

Introduction

This tutorial will guide you through the basics of navigating blockchain explorers. Blockchain explorers are search engines that allow you to explore the details of transactions, addresses, and blocks on a blockchain network.

Steps to Navigate Blockchain Explorers

  1. Choose a Blockchain Explorer

    • Ethereum
    • Avalanche
    • Fantom
    • Polygon
    • Binance Smart Chain
    • Bitcoin
    • Bitcoin Cash
    • Litecoin
    • Dogecoin
    • Zcash
    • eCash
    • Groestlcoin
    • Ripple

  2. Search for Transactions, Addresses, or Blocks

    Enter a transaction ID, wallet address, or block number/hash in the search bar and click "Search" or press Enter. The explorer will display detailed information about the queried item.

    Try a transaction ID on the Ethereum network: 0x0bf68b65f4ceff4e7c69b2c09adb7485044402b2228de34e3068f160df49ccb4

    Try a block on the Ethereum network: 445345

    Try an address on the ethereum network: 0xea4da2a6cfd871409ca4aa5c2f72fe62f6fb755d

  3. Understand the Data

    Learn to interpret the data provided by the explorer, such as:

    • Transaction details (e.g., input/output addresses, amounts, fees, and confirmations)
    • Address details (e.g., balance, transactions, and tokens)
    • Block details (e.g., timestamp, miner, and transactions included)
